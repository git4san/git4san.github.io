Frequently Asked Questions

How can I collaborate on research projects?

I'm always open to research collaborations with academic institutions, industry partners, and individual researchers. Please send me an email with details about your research interests, proposed collaboration scope, and timeline. Include relevant publications or project descriptions to help me understand the potential synergies.

Do you accept graduate students or postdocs?

Yes, I regularly accept motivated graduate students and postdoctoral researchers. Positions are typically available for students with strong backgrounds in computer science, cybersecurity, or machine learning. Please send your CV, research statement, and relevant coursework or experience. Funding availability varies by semester.

Are you available for speaking engagements?

I accept selected speaking invitations for conferences, workshops, and industry events related to cybersecurity, AI security, and emerging technologies. Please provide event details, expected audience, proposed topics, and timeline at least 2-3 months in advance. Note that I prioritize academic conferences and high-impact industry events.

Can you provide peer review for papers or grants?

I regularly serve as a reviewer for top-tier conferences and journals in cybersecurity and AI. For paper reviews, please ensure the work aligns with my expertise areas. For grant reviews, I participate in NSF and other agency panels. Due to high demand, please allow sufficient time for review completion.

Do you offer consulting services?

I provide limited consulting services for organizations working on cybersecurity challenges, AI security implementations, or research strategy development. Consulting opportunities must align with my research expertise and university policies. Please describe your specific needs, timeline, and engagement scope in your initial inquiry.

How do I schedule an office hours appointment?

Office hours are held Tuesday and Thursday from 2:00-4:00 PM, primarily for enrolled students. External visitors can schedule appointments by email at least one week in advance. Please specify the purpose of your visit and expected duration. Virtual meetings are also available for remote collaborators.
